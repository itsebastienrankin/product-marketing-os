## What This Document Is

Data-driven claims and proof points approved for external use in sales and marketing materials. These claims substantiate value propositions and messaging pillars with research-backed statistics and metrics.

**How to use:** Reference these claims when generating content. Always use the exact claim text and include required footnotes. Check `status` and `valid_until` before using any claim.

**How to update:** When adding new claims, fill every column. When a claim expires, change status to `expired` rather than deleting — this preserves the audit trail.

## Claim Status Guide

| Status | Meaning | Can Use? |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **active** | Verified, current, approved for use | Yes |
| **under-review** | Being verified or updated — do not use until confirmed | No |
| **expired** | Outdated or no longer defensible — do not use | No |
| **retired** | Replaced by a newer version of the same claim | No (use replacement) |

## Claim Strength Guide

| Strength | Meaning | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **verified-metric** | Independently measured or third-party validated | "G2 data: 2hr avg setup time (500+ reviews)" |
| **customer-reported** | Reported by customers in surveys, interviews, or case studies | "Teams save 10 hrs/week (customer survey, n=200)" |
| **internal-data** | From your own product analytics or internal measurement | "85% of teams actively using within 1 week (internal data)" |
| **analyst-cited** | From analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC, etc.) | "Named a Leader in Gartner Magic Quadrant 2025" |
| **directional** | Estimate or approximation — use with qualifying language | "Typically reduces onboarding time by weeks" |

## Usage Guidelines

### When to Use These Claims

- **[Theme 1]**: Use when addressing [e.g. cost concerns, operational efficiency, TCO, speed to value]
- **[Theme 2]**: Use when focusing on [e.g. tool consolidation, productivity, workflow improvement]
- **[Theme 3]**: Use when emphasizing [e.g. adoption rates, ease of use, low training burden]

### Claim Usage Rules

1. **Always check status** — Only use `active` claims. Never use `expired` or `under-review`.
2. **Always check valid_until** — If past the date, flag for review before using.
3. **Always include required footnotes** — If the claim text includes footnote language, it must appear.
4. **Respect "approved for" scope** — A claim approved for "sales" only should not appear in ads.
5. **Use exact text** — Don't paraphrase claims. Rounding, rewording, or extrapolating creates legal risk.
6. **Add "why it matters" context** — Never drop a number without explaining the business outcome.
7. **When in doubt, be conservative** — Understate rather than overstate. Use directional language if unsure.
