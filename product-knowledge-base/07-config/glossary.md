# Product Marketing Glossary

A comprehensive glossary of terms, acronyms, and definitions used across the product marketing knowledge base.

---

## General Marketing Terms

**Buyer Persona**  
A semi-fictional representation of your ideal customer based on market research and real data about your existing customers.

**Buyer's Journey**  
The process buyers go through to become aware of, evaluate, and purchase a new product or service. Typically: Awareness → Consideration → Decision.

**Campaign**  
A coordinated series of marketing activities designed to achieve a specific goal within a defined timeframe.

**Demand Generation**  
Marketing programs designed to create awareness and interest in your products and services.

**ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)**  
A description of the type of company that would benefit most from your product and provide significant value to your business.

**Lead Scoring**  
A methodology used to rank prospects based on their perceived value to the organization.

**MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)**  
A lead that has been deemed more likely to become a customer compared to other leads based on engagement and fit.

**SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)**  
A prospective customer that has been researched and vetted by both marketing and sales teams and is ready for the next stage in the sales process.

---

## Product Marketing Terms

**Battlecard**  
A one-page competitive reference document that helps sales teams understand how to position against a specific competitor.

**Competitive Intelligence**  
The process of gathering and analyzing information about competitors to inform strategic decisions.

**GTM (Go-to-Market)**  
The strategy of how a company brings a product to market, including target audience, positioning, channels, and tactics.

**Launch Tier**  
A classification system for product releases based on scope and importance (Tier 1 = major launch, Tier 4 = minor update).

**Messaging Framework**  
A structured document that outlines key messages, value propositions, and proof points for a product or company.

**Positioning**  
The unique space a product occupies in the mind of the target customer and how it's differentiated from competitors.

**Value Proposition**  
A clear statement that explains how your product solves customers' problems, delivers specific benefits, and why customers should buy from you instead of competitors.

---

## Sales Terms

**ACV (Annual Contract Value)**  
The average annualized revenue per customer contract.

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**  
The value of recurring revenue normalized for a single year.

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**  
The total sales and marketing cost required to acquire a new customer.

**Churn Rate**  
The percentage of customers who cancel or don't renew their subscriptions during a given time period.

**CLV/LTV (Customer Lifetime Value)**  
The predicted net profit attributed to the entire future relationship with a customer.

**Deal Cycle**  
The average length of time from first contact to closed deal.

**Pipeline**  
The collection of prospects and opportunities that sales is actively working.

**Win Rate**  
The percentage of opportunities that result in closed/won deals.

---

## Content & Creative Terms

**A/B Test**  
A randomized experiment comparing two versions of something to determine which performs better.

**Above the Fold**  
The portion of a webpage visible without scrolling; the most prominent real estate.

**Boilerplate**  
A standardized piece of company description text used consistently across press releases and official communications.

**Call to Action (CTA)**  
A prompt that encourages the reader to take a specific action.

**Creative Brief**  
A document that outlines the strategy and requirements for a creative project.

**Hero Image**  
The large, prominent image at the top of a webpage, typically in the header area.

**Social Proof**  
Evidence that other people have purchased and found value in a product (testimonials, case studies, customer logos).

**Use Case**  
A specific situation in which a product could potentially be used.

---

## Metrics & Analytics

**CAC:LTV Ratio**  
Customer Acquisition Cost compared to Lifetime Value; indicates business sustainability.

**Conversion Rate**  
The percentage of users who complete a desired action.

**CPL (Cost Per Lead)**  
The amount spent on marketing to acquire a single lead.

**CTR (Click-Through Rate)**  
The ratio of users who click on a specific link to the total number who view it.

**Engagement Rate**  
A metric that measures the level of engagement that content receives from an audience.

**Funnel Conversion**  
The percentage of prospects that move from one stage of the funnel to the next.

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)**  
A metric that measures customer loyalty and satisfaction.

**Open Rate**  
The percentage of email recipients who open a given email.

**ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)**  
Revenue generated for every dollar spent on advertising.

---

## Channel-Specific Terms

**Drip Campaign**  
A series of automated emails sent on a predetermined schedule.

**Nurture Sequence**  
A series of communications designed to build relationships with prospects over time.

**Paid Search / SEM**  
Search Engine Marketing; paid advertisements that appear on search engine results pages.

**Organic Search / SEO**  
Search Engine Optimization; unpaid search engine results.

**Retargeting**  
Advertising to people who have previously visited your website or engaged with your content.

**Webinar**  
A live or recorded online educational presentation.

---

## Technical Terms

**API (Application Programming Interface)**  
A way for two or more computer programs to communicate with each other.

**Integration**  
The connection of your product with other software tools.

**SaaS (Software as a Service)**  
Software licensing and delivery model where software is hosted centrally and licensed on a subscription basis.

**Tech Stack**  
The collection of technologies, tools, and platforms used by a company.

---

## Industry Acronyms

**B2B (Business-to-Business)**  
Companies that sell products or services to other businesses.

**B2C (Business-to-Consumer)**  
Companies that sell directly to consumers.

**CRM (Customer Relationship Management)**  
Software for managing company relationships and interactions with customers and prospects.

**ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)**  
Integrated management of main business processes, often in real-time.

**PLG (Product-Led Growth)**  
A go-to-market strategy that relies on product usage as the primary driver of acquisition, conversion, and expansion.

**ROI (Return on Investment)**  
A measure of the profitability of an investment.

**SMB (Small and Medium-sized Business)**  
Companies with fewer than 500-1000 employees (definition varies).

**TAM / SAM / SOM**  
Total Addressable Market / Serviceable Available Market / Serviceable Obtainable Market

---

## Internal Process Terms

**Launch Checklist**  
A comprehensive task list for executing a product launch.

**PMM (Product Marketing Manager)**  
The role responsible for product positioning, messaging, and go-to-market strategy.

**Stakeholder**  
Anyone with an interest in or influence over a project or initiative.

**Sprint**  
A time-boxed period (typically 1-2 weeks) for completing a set amount of work.

---

**Note**: This glossary is a living document. Add new terms as they become important to your organization and team.
