## What's in Each File

### 1. competitor-overview.md
**Strategic context about the competitor**

**Use when:** Planning competitive strategy, understanding market positioning  
**Read time:** 15-20 minutes  
**Update frequency:** Quarterly

**Contains:**
- Company profile (size, funding, market position)
- Their positioning strategy (which Al Ries framework do they use?)
- Product strengths and weaknesses by buyer role
- Win/loss patterns (when we win, when we lose)
- Deal timing and their sales tactics
- TCO comparison (total cost of ownership)

**Best for:** Sales managers, PMMs, AEs preparing for competitive deals

---

### 2. battlecard.md
**Quick tactical reference during active deals**

**Use when:** Competing against them in a live deal  
**Read time:** 5-10 minutes (scan for what you need)  
**Update frequency:** Monthly or as needed

**Contains:**
- Quick reference (win rate, their discount behavior)
- Objections by buyer role (Economic Buyer, Champion, Technical)
- Head-to-head comparison by your messaging pillars
- Trap-setting questions (expose their weaknesses)
- 30-second talk track (elevator pitch when competing)
- Win/loss patterns with specific scenarios

**Best for:** AEs, SEs in active competitive deals

---

### 3. objection-handling.md
**Detailed responses to common objections using A-R-P-C framework**

**Use when:** Prospect raises specific objection about them vs us  
**Read time:** Variable (search for specific objection)  
**Update frequency:** As new objections emerge

**Contains:**
- A-R-P-C method (Acknowledge → Reframe → Proof → Check)
- Objections by deal stage (Discovery, Evaluation, Technical Review, Close)
- Objections by buyer role (Economic, Champion, Technical)
- Full scripts with multiple response angles
- When to walk away (red flags where you should disengage)

**Framework example:**
- **A** - Acknowledge: "You're right - they do have [feature]"
- **R** - Reframe: "The question is: how often would you use it?"
- **P** - Proof: "[Customer] bought them for [feature], used it 5% of time"
- **C** - Check: "How critical is [feature] to your daily workflow?"

**Best for:** AEs handling objections, SEs in technical discussions

---

### 4. fud-playbook.md
**Track and counter their Fear/Uncertainty/Doubt tactics**

**Use when:** They're spreading negative info about you (true or false)  
**Read time:** 10-15 minutes  
**Update frequency:** Ongoing (add new FUD as you hear it)

**Contains:**
- FUD they spread about us (and how to counter each)
- Legitimate concerns we can raise about them (ethical positioning)
- Red flags when they cross the line (lies, price-dumping)
- Counter-FUD tactics (proactive positioning)
- Ethical boundaries (what we do/don't say)

**Best for:** AEs facing negative claims, sales leadership tracking competitor behavior

## How to Use This Folder

### For Sales Reps (AEs)

**Before a competitive deal:**
1. Read **battlecard.md** (10 mins) - get the quick hits
2. Scan **objection-handling.md** for likely objections
3. Note trap-setting questions to ask in discovery

**During a deal:**
1. Reference **battlecard** for quick answers
2. Use **objection-handling** for specific objections
3. Report new FUD to update **fud-playbook**

**After a win/loss:**
1. Share feedback with PMM to update files
2. Note what worked/didn't work

---

### For Sales Engineers (SEs)

**Use cases:**
- **Technical objections** → objection-handling.md (Technical Buyer section)
- **Feature comparison** → battlecard.md (Head-to-head by pillar)
- **Security/scalability FUD** → fud-playbook.md (FUD counters)
- **Demo planning** → competitor-overview.md (Product weaknesses to exploit)

---

### For Sales Managers

**Use cases:**
- **Deal strategy** → competitor-overview.md (Win/loss patterns)
- **Team training** → All files (objection role-plays, FUD training)
- **Tracking competitors** → competitor-overview.md (Monitor section)
- **Win/loss analysis** → Update all files with new intelligence

---

### For Product Marketing (PMMs)

**Responsibilities:**
- **Create** these files for each major competitor
- **Update** quarterly or when major changes occur
- **Train** sales team on new competitive intelligence
- **Track** win/loss data to improve positioning

## Which Competitors Need Folders?

**Priority 1: Create folders for these competitors**
- Direct competitors you see in >20% of deals
- Incumbents you're trying to displace
- Status quo (create a "Status Quo" folder for manual/multi-tool alternatives)

**Priority 2: Create folders for these**
- Different category solutions you compete with (15-20% of deals)
- Emerging competitors gaining traction

**Priority 3: Optional**
- Niche competitors you rarely see
- Legacy competitors losing market share

**Example (Notion):**
- Priority 1: Confluence, Status Quo (Google Drive + multi-tool)
- Priority 2: Coda, Asana (different category)
- Priority 3: Sharepoint, Monday.com

## Templates

**To create a new competitor folder:**

1. Copy template files from `/templates/sales-enablement/`:
   - competitor-overview-template.md
   - battlecard-template-IMPROVED.md
   - objection-handling-framework-template.md
   - fud-playbook-template.md

2. Create new folder: `sales-enablement/[Competitor Name]/`

3. Fill out files in this order:
   - competitor-overview.md (2-3 hours)
   - battlecard.md (1 hour)
   - objection-handling.md (2 hours)
   - fud-playbook.md (ongoing)

4. Train sales team on new competitor folder
