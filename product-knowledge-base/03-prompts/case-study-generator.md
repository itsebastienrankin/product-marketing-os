## STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK

### Positioning Principles
**Reference:** Use the segment context files provided (narrative-and-positioning.md, messaging-pillars.md, buyer-personas.md, market-segment-overview.md)

**Key positioning principles to apply:**
- **Context First:** Understand the market context this segment sees you in
- **Competitive Alternatives:** Reference what customers would use/do without your product (not just direct competitors)
- **Own One Position:** Emphasize the ONE position you own in this segment's mind
- **Simplify:** Focus on core differentiators, not feature lists
- **Customer as Hero:** The customer is the star; your product is the enabling tool

**Key for case studies:**
- Before: Describe their previous approach and pain points (status quo, competitive alternatives)
- After: Show how [PRODUCT NAME] changed their operations/results
- Use specific examples (time saved, errors eliminated, capabilities unlocked)
- Let customer voice carry the story through authentic quotes

### Writing Style & Principles
**Key emphasis for case studies:**
- **Customer as the hero:** Customer is the star, [PRODUCT NAME] is the supporting tool
- **Before/after narrative:** Clear problem → solution → results arc
- **Specific, quantified results:** Use real metrics and outcomes from the transcript
- **Natural quote integration:** Weave testimonials into body copy organically
- **Segment-specific messaging:** Align with messaging pillars and positioning for this segment

### Source Material: Interview Transcripts

**Your primary source:** The interview transcript provided by the user

**What to extract:**
- **Direct quotes:** Use actual customer language from the interview
- **Specific metrics:** Only numbers and outcomes mentioned by the customer
- **Pain points:** Problems they described before using [PRODUCT NAME]
- **Solutions:** How they explained using [PRODUCT NAME] to solve their challenges
- **Results:** Outcomes and improvements they shared

**Critical rules:**
- **Only use information from the provided transcript** - Never invent quotes, metrics, or details
- **Quote accurately:** Use customer's exact words when quoting
- **Verify metrics:** Only include numbers the customer explicitly stated
- **Natural voice:** Preserve the customer's authentic way of speaking
- **No extrapolation:** If they didn't say it, don't include it

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't reference other case studies or transcripts
- Don't invent testimonials or quotes
- Don't add metrics the customer didn't provide
- Don't make up "typical" results or industry benchmarks
- Don't assume outcomes that weren't stated

## TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

### Meta Details & URL Slug Requirements

| Field | Character Limit |
|---|---|
| **URL Slug** | Complete URL: [your-domain]/case-studies/[customer-name] - customer name separated by hyphens, e.g., "acme-software-case-study" |
| **Meta Title** | 60 characters max (including spaces, punctuation, and special characters) - compelling headline that highlights key outcome |
| **Meta Description** | 160 characters max (including spaces, punctuation, and special characters) - brief summary of the case study with key metrics |

### Content Structure Requirements

#### Introduction (H1 Title + Body Copy)
- **H1 Title**: 60 characters max (including spaces, punctuation, and special characters) - compelling headline that captures the main achievement
- **Body Copy**: 200-300 words - engaging opening that sets up the story and includes 3 bullet points highlighting key business outcomes

#### The Challenge (H2 + Body Copy)
- **H2 Heading**: 40 characters max (including spaces, punctuation, and special characters) - concise description of the main problem
- **Body Copy**: 300-400 words - detailed explanation of the challenges faced, with customer quotes naturally integrated into the narrative, ending with a key pull quote that highlights the challenge

#### The Solution (H2 + Body Copy)
- **H2 Heading**: 40 characters max (including spaces, punctuation, and special characters) - concise description of [PRODUCT NAME]'s approach
- **Body Copy**: 300-400 words - explanation of how [PRODUCT NAME] solved the problem, with customer quotes naturally integrated into the narrative, ending with a key pull quote that highlights the solution

#### The Results (H2 + Body Copy)
- **H2 Heading**: 40 characters max (including spaces, punctuation, and special characters) - concise description of the outcomes
- **Body Copy**: 300-400 words - detailed results and impact, with customer quotes naturally integrated into the narrative, ending with a key pull quote that highlights the results

#### Key Metrics (H2 + 3 Metrics)
- **H2 Heading**: Fixed as "Key Metrics"
- **3 Key Metrics**: Each with a heading (25 characters max including spaces, punctuation, and special characters) and description (100 characters max including spaces, punctuation, and special characters)

### Case Study Best Practices
- Follow blog-style format with H2 headings
- Naturally integrate quotes into body copy
- Ensure story flows logically with clear narrative arc
- Position customer as the hero, [PRODUCT NAME] as the enabling tool
- Emphasize the primary position you own in this segment's mind
- Reference competitive alternatives (status quo, DIY, competitors) that customer moved away from

### Claim Substantiation Requirements

**CRITICAL:** All claims must be defensible and substantiated. This is a legal and brand protection requirement.

**Defensible Claims:**
- Operational benefits: "Eliminated 25+ hours monthly of integration maintenance" (only if stated in transcript)
- Customer experience: "Increased customer satisfaction scores by 35%" (only if stated in transcript)
- Project-specific data: Use only data explicitly provided in interview transcript
- General improvements: "Improved operational efficiency," "Reduced complexity"

**X Avoid These Claims (Unless Verified):**
- Specific competitive comparisons: "Better than [competitor]," "Cheaper than [competitor]"
- Unverified metrics: Specific percentages or dollar amounts without source data from transcript
- Market claims: "Industry-leading," "Best-in-class" without substantiation
- Cost savings claims: Specific dollar amounts without verified data from transcript

**Guidelines:**
- When in doubt, be conservative - understate rather than overstate
- Focus on operational benefits rather than competitive comparisons
- Use source data only - don't extrapolate or make assumptions
- Emphasize complexity reduction and capabilities unlocked rather than specific cost savings
- **NEVER invent specific numbers** - No "6 hours per week," "50% faster," "2x cheaper" without explicit substantiation from transcript
- **Question every quantitative claim** - Ask "Where did this number come from?" (Must be in transcript)
- **Use general language** - "Reduces complexity" vs "Saves 3 hours per day" (unless customer stated the specific time)
- **Only use data explicitly provided** in interview transcript

## EXECUTION INSTRUCTIONS

### Copy Generation Process

1. **Review Source Documentation:**
   - Thoroughly analyze the interview transcript to understand:
     - Customer background, industry, size, and context
     - Specific challenges and solutions to highlight
     - Key metrics and outcomes to emphasize (only those stated in transcript)
     - Authentic quotes and language to preserve
   - Review segment context files provided:
     - narrative-and-positioning.md (positioning statement, competitive alternatives)
     - messaging-pillars.md (core messaging and value propositions)
     - buyer-personas.md (target personas and decision factors)
     - market-segment-overview.md (segment characteristics and GTM approach)

2. **Reference Examples:**
   - Review the case study structure example above to understand quality standards and structure

3. **Identify Customer Story Arc:**
   - Based on interview transcript, determine the narrative structure:
     - What was the primary challenge? (Connect to competitive alternatives from segment context)
     - How did [PRODUCT NAME] solve it? (Align with positioning statement and messaging pillars)
     - What were the measurable results? (Only use metrics from transcript)
     - What makes this story compelling for the target segment? (Reference buyer personas)

4. **Select Key Metrics:**
   - Choose the most impactful metrics that demonstrate [PRODUCT NAME]'s value
   - Only select metrics explicitly stated in the interview transcript
   - Align with segment-specific success metrics from buyer-personas.md

5. **Structure the Narrative:**
   - Organize the story following the required structure:
     - Introduction: Set up the story and highlight key outcomes (3 bullet points)
     - Challenge: Detail the problems they faced (reference competitive alternatives)
     - Solution: Explain how [PRODUCT NAME] solved them (align with positioning)
     - Results: Show the measurable impact (only transcript-stated metrics)
     - Key Metrics: Reinforce with specific numbers from transcript

6. **Create Content:**
   - Follow the technical specifications and character limits for each section
   - Include URL slug and SEO meta tags
   - Integrate quotes naturally into body copy (not standalone)
   - Reference segment positioning and messaging where appropriate

7. **Compare to Examples:**
   - Check your output against the structure example for format compliance
   - Verify character limits are respected
   - Ensure narrative flow matches the example structure

8. **Self-Review Loop:**
   - After creating each section, review against the quality checklist:
     - Verify character limits are respected
     - Ensure story flows logically with clear narrative arc
     - Check that quotes are naturally integrated into body copy
     - Validate [PRODUCT NAME] positioning and segment alignment
     - Confirm alignment with segment context files (positioning, messaging, personas)
     - Verify all metrics come from transcript only

9. **Refine and Optimize:**
   - Make adjustments based on quality checklist feedback
   - Ensure segment-specific messaging is woven throughout
   - Verify competitive alternatives are referenced (status quo they moved from)

10. **Explain Strategic Choices:**
    - How interview transcript influenced story selection and emphasis
    - Why you selected specific challenges and solutions to highlight
    - Which metrics you prioritized and why (must all be from transcript)
    - How segment context files informed positioning and messaging
    - Key narrative choices and their strategic rationale
