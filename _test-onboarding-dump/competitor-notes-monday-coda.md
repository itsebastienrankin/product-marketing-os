## Monday.com

### What they launched recently (as of ~Oct 2025)
- monday docs — their attempt at a docs product. It's fine. Not great. Feels bolted on.
- mondayDB — their new database thing? Haven't looked at it closely
- AI assistant — basically same as everyone else, summarize/generate/etc
- CRM product — they're trying to be a platform now, not just PM tool

### Where they win against us
- VISUAL. their UI is so colorful and satisfying. PMs love the dashboards
- Pricing is actually slightly cheaper at the low end for pure PM use cases
- Marketing spend is INSANE. they're everywhere. superbowl ads etc
- Their PM features are deeper — dependencies, gantt charts, workload views
- Construction, manufacturing, non-tech industries — they've built vertical solutions
- They don't have the "it's too flexible" problem. It's opinionated, which some teams prefer

### Where we win against Monday
- Docs/wiki — ours is 10x better, not even close
- Knowledge management — they don't really have this
- Flexibility — you can build anything in Notion. Monday is more rigid
- Developer/technical teams — Monday feels like a tool for managers
- AI — our AI is better because it has context across docs+projects+wiki
- Free tier — ours is actually usable, theirs is a joke
- Brand perception in tech — Notion is "cool", Monday is "corporate"

### Key deals we lost to Monday (from sales team)
1. **Acme Manufacturing** (500 ppl) — they wanted a PM tool with timesheets. We don't do timesheets. Monday does.
2. **Bright Health** (1200 ppl) — their ops team loved Monday's dashboards. Our databases can do the same thing but it takes more setup.
3. **RetailCo** (2000 ppl) — already using Monday for PM, didn't want to move. We tried to sell wiki alongside but they didn't want to pay for two tools.

### Random observations
- Monday stock price has been rough. They might get acquired? Rumors about Salesforce.
- Their enterprise motion is getting more serious — hired a bunch of ex-Atlassian people
- They're at ~225K customers last earnings call. We don't disclose customers the same way but "4M teams" is our number

## Coda

### Overview
Ok honestly Coda is probably our closest true competitor. They think about things the same way we do — docs as the foundation, databases built in, extensible.

### Where Coda wins
- **Formulas and automation** — their formula language is more powerful. Power users LOVE it. It's like Excel on steroids inside a doc.
- **Packs** — their integration model is slick. Connect to any API and pull data into your doc. We have integrations too but theirs feel more native for "docs that do things"
- **Ritual features** — standup bots, meeting docs with auto-pull from calendar, voting/reactions. Fun collaborative stuff.
- **Pricing** — doc-maker vs viewer pricing model. Cheaper for large orgs with many viewers.

### Where we win
- **Brand & scale** — we're way bigger. More templates, bigger community, more content about us
- **Wiki/knowledge base** — Coda doesn't really have a wiki product. It's all docs. For company-wide knowledge management, we win.
- **Design** — Notion is just prettier. Sorry Coda.
- **Project management** — our PM features have gotten way better. Coda's is an afterthought.
- **AI breadth** — our AI works across the whole workspace. Coda's AI is more doc-scoped.
- **Enterprise readiness** — we have more enterprise features (SCIM, advanced permissions, audit log). Coda is still catching up.

### Coda deals analysis
- We almost NEVER lose to Coda in enterprise. It's always mid-market or small team deals.
- When we lose it's usually: 1 power user who loves Coda formulas and evangelizes internally
- Coda's growth has slowed. They did layoffs in 2024. But the product is still good.

### What Coda is saying about us
From their sales team (per competitive intel from Jake):
- "Notion is just pretty Google Docs"
- "Notion doesn't let you build real automations"
- "Try building a complex workflow in Notion — you'll hit walls"

These are somewhat true tbh. Our automation story is weak compared to Coda. But most teams don't need complex automations so it's a niche advantage.

## Confluence (quick notes — need a real doc for this)

this deserves its own doc because confluence is our #1 competitor in enterprise. just dumping some thoughts:

- Atlassian is bundling HARD. Jira + Confluence + Loom (they bought Loom!) for one price
- The bundling makes it almost free to add Confluence if you're already paying for Jira
- Their new AI features (Atlassian Intelligence) are getting better
- Confluence Cloud is way better than old server version. Don't underestimate them.
- HOWEVER: everyone still hates Confluence. Go on Twitter/Reddit and it's all complaints.
- Our best angle: "Life's too short for Confluence" (not approved messaging lol but the vibe)
- Migration from Confluence is our most common inbound. We need to make this easier.

Enterprise deals where Confluence is incumbent:
- Takes 6-9 months to displace
- Usually starts with one team, then spreads
- IT is the gatekeeper — need to get security review done early
- Jira integration is the #1 blocker. If eng team needs Jira, they want Confluence because "it connects to Jira"
- Our answer: API integrations, embed Jira issues in Notion, but it's not as tight as native Atlassian

## Other competitors (not worth full docs yet)

**Slite** — small, focused on internal knowledge base. Nice product but tiny. Not a real threat.
**Almanac** — async docs tool. Interesting but niche. Mostly remote-first companies.
**Slab** — knowledge base. Decent search. But no PM features, no databases. Limited.
**Clickup** — trying to be everything. Their docs feature is bad. PM features are ok. Messy product.
**Basecamp** — different philosophy (simplicity). Not really competing with us anymore.

None of these come up in deals regularly. If they do, it's usually alongside us (customer using Notion + Slite for different things, which is weird but it happens).
