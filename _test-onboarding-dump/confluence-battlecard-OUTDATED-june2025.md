## WHEN WE WIN

**Signal phrases from prospects that indicate we'll win:**
- "Our team hates Confluence"
- "Nobody can find anything"
- "We need something modern"
- "We want one tool for docs, wiki, AND projects"
- "Our wiki is a graveyard"
- "We're tired of needing an admin just to change a page"

**Our advantages:**
1. **User experience / design** — Notion is beautiful. Confluence looks like 2010. This matters for adoption.
2. **Flexibility** — Databases, pages, wikis, projects all in one. Confluence is pages and that's basically it.
3. **AI search** — Our AI-powered search is genuinely better. Confluence search is notoriously terrible.
4. **Adoption** — Teams WANT to use Notion. Confluence is the tool people are forced to use.
5. **Self-service** — No admin required. Anyone can create, organize, and share. Confluence needs dedicated admins.
6. **Modern collaboration** — Real-time editing, comments, mentions, integrations. Confluence is catching up but still behind.
7. **Templates** — Our template gallery (community + official) is massive. Confluence templates are limited and rigid.
8. **All-in-one** — ~~wait we're not saying this anymore~~ I mean "connected workspace" — docs + wiki + PM in one tool

**Proof points:**
- Loom: consolidated 5 tools including Confluence, 40% faster onboarding
- CoreTech: switched from Confluence, "finally I can find things"
- Apex Retail: "Our team actually USES Notion. Nobody used Confluence."

## WHEN WE LOSE

**Signal phrases that indicate trouble:**
- "We're heavily invested in Jira"
- "IT needs to approve all vendors"
- "We need deep Jira integration"
- "Budget is tight / Confluence is basically free with our Jira license"
- "We have 10,000+ employees and need proven enterprise scale"
- "We need HIPAA compliance" ← instant disqualifier, we don't have it

**Their advantages:**
1. **Jira integration** — Native, deep, seamless. Our Jira integration is ok but not comparable. This is their #1 weapon.
2. **Bundle pricing** — Jira + Confluence + Loom for one price. Confluence is nearly free if you're on Jira. We can't compete on cost.
3. **Enterprise scale** — Atlassian has customers with 50K+ users. We're getting there but they have more proof points.
4. **HIPAA compliance** — They have it. We don't. Healthcare deals are often disqualified.
5. **Macro ecosystem** — Atlassian Marketplace has thousands of add-ons. Our integration ecosystem is growing but smaller.
6. **IT familiarity** — IT teams know Atlassian. We're still building enterprise credibility.
7. **Analyst rankings** — They're in Gartner/Forrester for work management. We're not (yet?).

## OBJECTION HANDLING

**"Confluence is free/cheap with our Jira license."**
→ "Free isn't free if nobody uses it. The cost of a tool that teams avoid is higher than the cost of a tool they embrace — in wasted time, duplicated effort, and knowledge loss. What's it costing you when new hires can't find what they need?"

**"We need Jira integration."**
→ "We integrate with Jira — you can embed issues, sync projects, and link to tickets. For most teams, this covers 80%+ of the use case. The question is: do you want a wiki that's great because of its Jira integration, or a wiki that's great because it's actually a great wiki?"
*[This is a bit combative. Soften for risk-averse IT buyers.]*

**"Notion is just for startups."**
→ "50% of Fortune 500 companies use Notion. [Insert relevant enterprise customer]. We recently closed [Redwood Financial, 6000 employees / Samsung / GE — pick the most relevant]. Enterprise is our fastest-growing segment."

**"Can Notion handle 5,000+ users?"**
→ "Yes — we have customers with 10,000+ employees on Notion. Our Enterprise plan includes SSO, SCIM, audit logs, data residency, and a dedicated CSM. Happy to connect you with a reference customer at similar scale."

**"We've tried to move off Confluence before and it was too painful."**
→ "Migration technology has improved. We have an automated Confluence importer plus dedicated migration support for Enterprise. Most customers complete migration in 4-6 weeks. And the good news: this is a chance to clean house. You don't need to bring everything — just the knowledge that matters."

**"Our engineering team needs Confluence for technical docs."**
→ Tread carefully here. If eng is the primary user and Jira is central, this is a hard fight. Best angle: "Start with the non-engineering teams who don't need Jira integration. Prove the value there, then let it spread organically." Don't try to displace Confluence for eng teams that are deeply integrated with Jira — you'll lose.
