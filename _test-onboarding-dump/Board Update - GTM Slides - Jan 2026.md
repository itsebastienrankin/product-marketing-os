## SLIDE 3: Competitive Landscape Shift

**What changed in H2 2025:**

**🔴 Microsoft (NEW THREAT)**
- M365 Copilot bundling is killing us in enterprise
- SharePoint + Teams + Loop = "free" workspace
- We lost 4 enterprise deals to the Microsoft bundle in H2
- Need: "Notion alongside Microsoft" positioning and ROI story

**🟡 Atlassian (PERSISTENT THREAT)**
- Confluence Cloud improving + Loom acquisition
- Jira + Confluence + Loom bundle at one price
- Still our #1 competitor in enterprise displacement deals
- BUT: organic dissatisfaction with Confluence remains our best inbound source

**🟢 Monday.com (STABLE)**
- Expanding beyond PM into docs/wiki
- More of a mid-market competitor
- Their docs product is still immature
- Not a major threat in enterprise

**🟢 Coda (DECLINING)**
- Layoffs in 2024, growth slowed
- Still wins niche power-user deals
- Not investing in enterprise motion
- Less of a concern than 12 months ago

**Speaker Notes:** The board cares about Microsoft. That's the conversation they want to have. Atlassian is a known quantity. Frame Microsoft as an emerging pattern we're watching and addressing, not a crisis. Two data points don't make a trend, but the pipeline says more Microsoft encounters are coming in H1.

## SLIDE 4: H1 2026 GTM Priorities

**Three bets for H1:**

**1. "Notion + Microsoft" coexistence play**
- New positioning: "Notion is where knowledge lives. M365 is where communication happens."
- Integration roadmap: deeper Teams, Outlook, SharePoint connectors (coordinate with product)
- Sales enablement: "Selling alongside Microsoft" playbook for AEs
- Target: reduce Microsoft-related losses from 30% of enterprise pipeline to under 15%

**2. AI as the enterprise differentiator**
- Notion AI included in Enterprise plan — our competitors charge extra or have weaker AI
- "Your company's AI brain" narrative — only works if all knowledge is in Notion
- AI search as the Confluence killer: "Stop searching. Start asking."
- Target: AI mentioned in 80%+ of enterprise deal cycles (currently ~45%)

**3. Mid-market PLG → Sales-assist motion**
- Our mid-market overperformance is product-led teams converting to paid
- Invest in the "shadow IT → sanctioned tool" motion (like the Acme Corp situation)
- New role: Product-Led Sales team (3-4 reps) to accelerate organic-to-enterprise conversion
- Target: 15% increase in PLG-to-enterprise conversion rate

**Speaker Notes:** These three things are what PMM is focused on. David will cover the sales org changes and quota planning. Sarah will cover the positioning and messaging work for each of these bets. If the board asks about product roadmap dependencies (especially the Microsoft integrations), punt to [CPO name] — that's a product conversation.
