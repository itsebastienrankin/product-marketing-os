## Vision Statement

Notion is the connected workspace where teams build, plan, and collaborate — replacing the patchwork of disconnected tools that slow modern teams down.

~~Notion is the all-in-one workspace that replaces your wiki, project management tool, and docs.~~

> NOTE from Sarah: we moved away from "all-in-one" positioning in Q2. Stop using it. I've told sales three times. The new frame is "connected workspace." All-in-one sounds like we're a jack of all trades. Connected workspace implies depth + integration.

## Target Segments

### 1. Mid-Market (200-2000 employees)
**Primary buyer:** Head of Operations or Chief of Staff
**Champion:** Team leads who are already using Notion for their team

Key pain points:
- Tool sprawl (avg company this size uses 87 SaaS tools per Okta report)
- Knowledge silos between departments
- Onboarding new hires takes forever because tribal knowledge lives in people's heads
- They're paying for Confluence + Asana + Google Docs + some wiki thing and none of them talk to each other

**Win theme:** "One platform, not ten tabs"

### 2. Enterprise (2000+ employees)

**Primary buyer:** CIO or VP of IT
**Champion:** Innovation team or digital workplace team

Enterprise is different because:
- They care about security/compliance first, functionality second
- Longer sales cycles (6-9 months avg)
- Need SSO, SCIM, audit logs — table stakes stuff
- Usually displacing Confluence (80% of enterprise deals per Jake on the sales team)

**Win theme:** Still working on this. Something about "structured flexibility" — the idea that IT gets governance controls while teams get the flexibility to build their own workflows.

> David's feedback from the offsite: "We need to nail the enterprise narrative. Right now it sounds like we're pitching the same thing to a 50-person startup and a 5,000-person bank. That can't be right."

### 3. Startups / Small Teams (under 200)
Honestly we don't invest much in marketing here. Product-led growth handles it. Free tier → team plan conversion. The messaging is basically "stop using Google Docs for everything" and it works.

Not a priority for PMM this year per the H1 plan.

## Competitive Positioning

See the battlecard doc — actually I think it's outdated. Last updated was like June 2025. Need to redo it after Monday.com launched their docs feature and Coda did that AI thing.

**Quick and dirty positioning vs key competitors:**

**vs Confluence:**
- We're modern, they're legacy
- We're flexible (databases, docs, wikis in one), they're rigid
- Their search is terrible and everyone knows it
- BUT they have deep Jira integration and that's hard to compete with in eng-heavy orgs
- New angle: "You shouldn't need a Confluence admin to update a page"

**vs Monday.com:**
- They're project management first, trying to expand into docs/wiki
- We're docs/knowledge first, with strong project management
- Our depth in both areas is stronger
- Their UI is more colorful/visual which appeals to creative teams (acknowledge this)

**vs Coda:**
- Honestly Coda is the most similar to us
- They lean harder into "docs that do things" — automation, packs
- We lean into "connected workspace" — broader use case coverage
- They're smaller, less brand awareness
- When we lose to Coda it's usually power users who want deeper formula/automation stuff

**vs Google Docs/Drive:**
- "You're already paying for it" is the hardest objection
- Our angle: Google Docs is a file system. Notion is a knowledge system. Files ≠ knowledge.
- Structure, databases, relations between information — Google can't do this
- Collab is comparable now so don't lean on that
