## SLIDE 2: The Problem

**Your team's knowledge is everywhere. And nowhere.**

- The average mid-size company uses **87 SaaS tools** (Source: Okta 2024 Businesses at Work report)
- **65%** of employees say they can't find the information they need to do their job (Source: ???  — I think this is from a Notion-commissioned survey? Check with comms)
- Teams spend **5+ hours/week** searching for information across tools

[Visual: messy collage of Slack, email, Google Docs, Confluence, Asana, Jira logos all overlapping]

**Speaker Notes:** This is your empathy slide. Pause here. Ask "does this feel familiar?" — get them nodding. If they say "yeah, we use Confluence for wiki, Asana for projects, Google Docs for everything else" that's your opening. The goal is to get them to articulate the pain. Do NOT jump to the solution yet. Let them sit with the pain for a sec.

## SLIDE 5: How Teams Use Notion

**Three ways teams transform their work:**

**1. Team Wiki**
- Company knowledge in one searchable place
- No more "where's that doc?"
- Auto-verified pages so content stays fresh

**2. Project Management**
- Tasks, sprints, timelines — without the bloat
- Connected to the docs and context that matter
- "Project management that doesn't feel like project management"

**3. Docs & Collaboration**
- Real-time collab (Google Docs quality, finally)
- Embed anything: Figma, Loom, code blocks, databases
- Templates that teams actually use

**Speaker Notes:** Tailor this to whatever you learned in discovery. If they said "our wiki is a mess," lead with #1. If they said "we're drowning in Asana," lead with #2. If they said "we just use Google Docs for everything," lead with #3. Never present all three with equal weight — pick their pain and lean into it.

## SLIDE 6: AI Built In, Not Bolted On

**Notion AI understands your workspace**

- **Ask anything:** "What did the design team decide about the rebrand?" → instant answer pulled from meeting notes, docs, and projects
- **Summarize:** Turn a 40-page PRD into an exec summary
- **Generate:** Draft from your existing knowledge, in your voice
- **Autofill:** Database properties populated by AI (magic)

*Unlike ChatGPT or generic AI tools, Notion AI has full context of your team's actual work.*

**Speaker Notes:** The key differentiator vs generic AI: context. ChatGPT doesn't know your company. Notion AI does because it sits on top of all your team's knowledge. If they ask "how is this different from just using ChatGPT?" — that's your answer. It's the difference between asking a stranger vs asking a team member who's been in every meeting and read every doc.

Big asterisk: AI features are add-on pricing. Don't bring up pricing here. If they ask, say "we'll cover packaging later" and keep moving.

## SLIDE 11: Pricing Overview

**Simple, transparent pricing**

| Plan | Price | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Individuals, trying it out |
| Plus | $10/user/mo | Small teams getting organized |
| Business | $18/user/mo | Companies scaling with Notion |
| Enterprise | Custom | Large orgs, advanced security needs |

*AI add-on: +$10/user/mo (included in Enterprise)*

**Speaker Notes:** DO NOT SHOW THIS SLIDE if you can avoid it. Pricing conversations should happen late in the sales cycle, ideally after a pilot. If someone asks about pricing early, talk about "investment" not "cost" and pivot to value. "Let me show you the ROI first and then pricing will make a lot more sense."

If forced to show pricing: emphasize that Enterprise includes AI. That's the upsell hook. Business plan customers pay extra for AI, Enterprise gets it bundled.

Discount authority: up to 15% for annual commit. Anything over 15%, get VP approval. Over 25%, don't even ask (we don't do it).
