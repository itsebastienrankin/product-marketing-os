## Exercise 2: Why Notion over [competitor]?

Three breakout groups, one competitor each:

### Group 1 (Sarah + Marcus): vs Confluence
**In one sentence:** "Notion is the wiki people actually use."
- Ease of use is the killer differentiator
- Beautiful design → higher adoption → stickier
- Search that works (AI-powered)
- Flexible structure (databases + pages + teamspaces) vs rigid page tree
- The "Confluence admin" tax — you need a dedicated admin. Notion you don't.
- CAUTION: don't trash-talk Confluence directly. Customers love to trash it themselves. Let them say it, we just nod.

**Things Confluence does better (be honest):**
- Jira integration (duh)
- Macros ecosystem (we have /blocks but less extensive)
- Page-level restrictions (our permissions are page-level too but less granular)
- Proven at massive scale (50K+ users)

### Group 2 (David + Jake): vs Monday.com
**In one sentence:** "Notion is where work gets done AND documented. Monday is just tracking."
- Monday = colorful task tracker. Notion = knowledge + projects + docs
- They're PM-first trying to be a platform. We're workspace-first with PM built in.
- Their docs feature is an afterthought. Ours IS the product.
- When teams need to make decisions (not just track tasks), they need context. Context = docs + data + discussion in one place. That's Notion.

**Things Monday does better (be honest):**
- Visual dashboards and reporting
- Time tracking, workload views, resource management
- Industry-specific templates (construction, manufacturing)
- Simpler for pure PM use cases — less setup
- Marketing spend / brand awareness in non-tech industries

### Group 3 (Jamie + Lisa): vs Microsoft 365 / SharePoint
**In one sentence:** "Microsoft is where files live. Notion is where knowledge lives."
- Files ≠ knowledge. SharePoint is a file system with a UI. Notion is a knowledge system.
- Teams/Slack is communication. Notion is documentation + collaboration. Different jobs.
- Loop is Microsoft's answer to Notion but it's... not good? Early days. Keep watching.
- The bundling problem: M365 is "free" if you're already paying for Office. Hard to justify Notion cost on top.

**UNSOLVED PROBLEM: How do we sell alongside Microsoft?**
- "Notion AND Microsoft, not Notion OR Microsoft"
- Position Notion as the knowledge layer that sits on top of M365 communication
- Integration play: "We connect to Teams, Outlook, SharePoint — we make your Microsoft investment better"
- Need product to build deeper Microsoft integrations for this to be credible
- **Action item: Sarah to draft "Notion + Microsoft" positioning doc by end of October** ← this didn't happen. Moving to H1 2026 priorities.

## Exercise 3: Messaging Tiers

**Tier 1 — Company level (every audience)**
"Notion is the connected workspace where teams build, plan, and share knowledge — in one place."

**Tier 2 — Segment level**

| Segment | Core Message |
|---------|-------------|
| Mid-Market | "Replace your stack of disconnected tools with one workspace teams actually want to use." |
| Enterprise | "Give every team flexibility to work their way, with the governance IT requires." |
| Startups | "The only tool that grows with you — from first hire to IPO." |

**Tier 3 — Persona level**

| Persona | Message | Emotional Hook |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| Head of Ops / CoS | "Stop paying for tools nobody uses." | Frustration with waste |
| CIO / IT | "Flexibility with guardrails." | Control anxiety |
| Team Lead | "Your team's brain, organized." | Knowledge chaos |
| End User | "One tab instead of ten." | Daily annoyance |
| CFO | "$X saved by consolidating Y tools." | Cost pressure |
