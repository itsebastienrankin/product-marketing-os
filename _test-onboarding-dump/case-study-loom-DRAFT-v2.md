## The Challenge

Before Notion, Loom's operations team was managing work across five different tools:

1. **Confluence** — company wiki and documentation
2. **Asana** — project and task management
3. **Google Docs** — general documents, meeting notes, drafts
4. **Airtable** — databases for tracking initiatives, OKRs, hiring pipeline
5. **Slite** — some teams used this for quick notes and daily standups

"We had knowledge everywhere and nowhere," said [NAME — need to confirm attribution], Loom's Head of Operations. "A new hire would ask 'where's the onboarding doc?' and the answer was 'which part? The Confluence page has the overview, Asana has the checklist, Google Docs has the department-specific stuff, and honestly some of it is just in people's Slack messages.'"

**Key pain points:**
- New hire onboarding took 3+ weeks on average
- Engineers estimated spending 5-7 hours/week searching for information
- Tool costs: ~$380K/year across all five platforms (not including the hidden cost of context-switching)
- No single source of truth — conflicting information across tools
- Every team had their own preferred tool, creating silos

## The Solution

Loom's ops team ran a 60-day evaluation of Notion, starting with a pilot of 40 people across Engineering, Product, and Operations.

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2): Foundation**
- Migrated company wiki from Confluence (automated import + manual cleanup)
- Set up teamspaces for each department
- Created core templates: meeting notes, project briefs, decision docs

**Phase 2 (Weeks 3-4): Expand**
- Migrated project tracking from Asana into Notion databases
- Built custom OKR tracking (previously in Airtable)
- Engineering team moved sprint planning into Notion
- ~~Created integrations between Notion and Slack/GitHub~~ [actually I don't think they did this, remove]

**Phase 3 (Weeks 5-8): Rollout**
- Company-wide rollout with department-by-department training
- Shut down Confluence, Asana, Slite accounts
- Kept Airtable for one specific use case (recruiting pipeline — later migrated too)
- Notion champions program: 1 power user per team to help with adoption

## Key Takeaways

1. **Start with a focused pilot** — 40 people across 3 teams, not a big-bang rollout
2. **Migrate wiki first** — it's the stickiest use case and gets people into the tool daily
3. **Champions program works** — having one power user per team accelerated adoption more than formal training
4. **Don't migrate everything at once** — they kept Airtable for 2 months before fully switching
5. **Executive sponsorship matters** — Joe personally used Notion for company all-hands, signaling commitment
