### Third Paragraph: The Details
Explain what's being announced in more detail. Key features, benefits, or program details.

A quote from company executive that provides vision, strategy, or human element. Should NOT just repeat facts from the release.

"[Quote that provides strategic context or customer benefit perspective]," said [Name, Title, Company]. "[Optional second sentence with additional insight]."

## Style Requirements

### AP Style Guidelines:
- Use Associated Press style for dates, numbers, titles
- Avoid jargon and marketing speak
- Third-person perspective only
- Present tense for quotes, past tense for news
- Spell out acronyms on first use

### Best Practices:
- Keep to one page if possible (400-600 words)
- Most important information first (inverted pyramid)
- Make it newsworthy (not just promotional)
- Include relevant data and metrics
- Quotes should sound human, not corporate
- Avoid superlatives without proof ("best," "leading," etc.)
- Include specific, concrete details

### SEO Considerations:
- Include target keywords naturally
- Front-load important terms
- Link to relevant pages
- Use descriptive anchor text

## Newsworthiness Checklist
Does this announcement have:
- [ ] Market impact (new category, significant milestone)
- [ ] Customer benefit (solves real problem)
- [ ] Timeliness (why now matters)
- [ ] Uniqueness (first, only, biggest)
- [ ] Data/proof (metrics, customers, validation)

## Distribution Guidance
**Recommended for**:
- Major product launches
- Significant partnerships
- Company milestones (funding, acquisition, 100th customer)
- Industry firsts
- Award wins

**NOT recommended for**:
- Minor feature updates
- Routine company news
- Content that's only promotional

## Output Format
Provide complete press release ready for distribution, formatted properly with all sections included.
